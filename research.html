Research

Methods For Securing Commercial Dies In Silicon Substrates For Heterogenous Integration

Advisor: Zhihong Chen, Peter Bermel | Purdue University Brick Nano Technology Center

Our research project aims to prototype and refine specialized 2.5D Heterogeneous Integration (HI) processes for embedding millimeter-level modular commercial chiplets, specifically d-latch memory microchips and photodiodes, onto a silicon-based interposer for low-earth orbit satellite system applications. We utilize the unique material characteristics of silicon and the advantages of 2.5D HI to optimize our system in reducing interconnect lengths, lowering power consumption, and improving signal integrity. The process flow starts with building a foundation layer by pattern-etching of the carrier substrate, depositing the gaps with a seed layer of copper and growing the metal through electroplating to secure the chiplets, and depositing an extra insulation layer to protect the foundation layer. Following, we build an interconnect layer by depositing photoresist and copper, patterning connections, metallizing traces, and performing a final lift-off to establish connection pins. Through extensive prototyping, efficient identifying and solving of challenges, and optimizations made to increase fabrication speeds, surface cleanliness and evenness, we aim to provide satellite manufacturers with a reliable and cost-effective 2.5D packaging solution for space electronics, contributing to consistent and efficient space endeavors.
